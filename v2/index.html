Work

Over the past four years I have acted as a photojournalist, videographer, web developer, IT manager and editor-in-chief of the CU Independent. Over that time I was proud to build a new website and brand for the CUI.

I have also had the privilege to do a number of fantastic internships. For two summers I worked with former National Geographic Director of Photography Rich Clarkson at Clarkson Creative. I started at the small multimedia firm straight out of high school and learned very quickly about the business of journalism and how photojournalists work. I spent one summer learning about community journalism at The Greeley Tribune, where I gained a great respect for deadlines. More recently, I was honored to be chosen as one of 15 college journalists from around the country to participate in the first ever PBS News Hour InaugBlog. And last summer I spent 10 weeks in Washington D.C. with the Scripps Howard Foundation Wire producing national multimedia pieces. At Scripps I got my first taste of data journalism and learned the importance of good formatting.

In May I plan on graduating from the University of Colorado with a Bachelor of Science in journalism, a minor in Technology, Arts & Media, an advertising certificate and strong interest in business. I've taken computer science courses including one on iOS app development, business courses in marketing and entrepreneurialism and of course traditional journalism courses in ethics and legal principles.

After graduation I hope to join a forward-thinking newsroom where I can tell important stories. If you are looking for an eager journalist who is ready to hit the ground running, please get in touch with me via email or Twitter below. And please feel free to check out my resume.

About me

I'm a visual storyteller. Because what is journalism if not storytelling?

I think that photos, video, data viz and web widgets have been vastly underutilized on the Net. I want to fix that. This is not to draw away from the importance of text, words are still the most efficient way to communicate complicated information. But, there are other methods to tell stories.

I'm a firm believer in transmedia journalism and investigating new revenue models for our business.

And I want to disrupt. Because change is good. It may not be comfortable, but if you embrace it, if you truly throw your weight into the winds of change, there's no telling what we can accomplish.
